UK Immigration Visa Eligibility Consultation

Immigration decisions are rarely black and white — and getting it right matters. This is a paid, in-depth advice session designed to assess whether you may qualify for a UK visa, such as a Spouse visa, Skilled Worker visa, or Indefinite Leave to Remain (ILR). We’ll take the time to understand your situation, explain the relevant requirements, and give you tailored guidance — so you’re informed, prepared, and not misled by generic answers.

What is it we do?

At Mannah Trust Consultancy Services, we support immigration law firms by delivering a consistent flow of pre-screened visa candidates who are ready for consultation.

Skilled Worker Visa

Formerly known as the Tier 2 General work visa, this is for skilled migrant workers who wish to work in the UK and have been sponsored by an employer who holds a valid Skilled Worker sponsor licence.

Global Talent Visa

The Global Talent visa is for non-British/ROI individuals deemed the best and brightest in their respective industries who want to immigrate to the UK.

UK Standard Visitor Visa

With a UK Standard Visitor visa, you have the right to visit the UK as a visitor (for leisure), for business purposes or for another reason such as the receipt of private medical treatment.

UK Self-Sponsorship

Self-Sponsorship route refers to an application to come and work in the UK for your own business This can lead to permanent residency and British citizenship for yourself and your family.

British Citizenship

British citizenship gives you the right to live, work, and travel in the UK without any restrictions, by granting the status of a British citizen or National.

Indefinite Leave to Remain

Indefinite leave to remain is the final road to settlement that allows immigrants to stay in the UK without any time limit. Immigrants with an ILR are free to take up employment or study in the UK.

Frequently asked questions

Is There a Self Sponsorship Visa Route UK?

Self-sponsorship in the UK is not a specific type of visa, but rather a way for business owners to stay/live in the UK to run their active trading business. This involves using a Skilled Worker visa, combined with having a sponsor licence for your own UK-based business.

How do I apply for a Standard Visitor Visa UK?

Applications for a Standard Visitor Visa UK are made online unless you are from North Korea. You will need to have your biometric information, such as fingerprints and photographs, taken at your local Visa Application Centre. Our lawyers have a proven record of success securing Standard Visitor Visas for clients and will guide you through each step of the process.

How long will it take to receive a Standard Visitor Visa UK?

Unless you apply using either the priority or super-priority service (which are not available in all countries), it can take an average of up to three weeks to find out if your Standard Visitor Visa UK has been granted. If the priority or super-priority services are available in your country, subject to paying an additional fee, you are likely to receive a decision within five working days or by the next working day, respectively.

What is the Skilled Worker route visa?

At the end of 2020, the Home Office made changes to a new points-based immigration system. The Skilled Worker route opened for applications and replaced the Tier 2 (General work visa) route. While there are a number of similarities between Tier 2 (General) and the Skilled Worker route, changes have been introduced to allow employers to sponsor a wider workforce from outside the UK.
